The previous site had accumulated too many small decisions. Some were useful at the time. Some were experiments. Together they became friction.

The rebuild has a simpler job: make a place where research, notes, and contact details are easy to trust. The homepage can carry the atmosphere. The article pages should get out of the way. The keys page should be boring in the best possible sense.

I want the site to feel like a field desk rather than a product landing page. A console for orientation. Case files for research. A paper-like archive for longer writing. Haku remains because every desk needs one detail that refuses to become corporate.

This version is a foundation, not a trophy case. The proof should arrive over time through better posts, better writeups, and cleaner records of what I am learning.
